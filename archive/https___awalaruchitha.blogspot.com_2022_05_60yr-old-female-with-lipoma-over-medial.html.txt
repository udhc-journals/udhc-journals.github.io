A 60yr old female came to hospital with swelling over medial aspect of right
knee since 3years 
HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS 
Patient was apparently asymptomatic 3years back and then she developed a
swelling which is insidious in onset and gradually progressive. Patient has no
history of pain,no discharge,weight loss,fever ,trauma 
HISTORY OF PAST ILLNESS 
Patient has no history of hypertension, diabetes
mellitus,asthma,epilepsy,cardiovascular disease
PERSONAL HISTORY 
Married 
Apetite: normal 
Diet:mixed 
Bowels:regular 
Micturition:normal 
No known allergies 
No addictions 
No known family history
GENERAL EXAMINATION 
No pallor , no cyanosis,lymphadenopathy,malnutrition,icterus,clubbing of
fingers or toes ,edema of feet 
VITALS:
Temperature: 98.7F 
Pulse rate:80/min
Bp: 100/70mmhg 
Spo2:99percent 
SYSTEMIC EXAMINATION 
CVS
No thrills , cardiac murmurs
Cardiac sounds:S1S2  
RESPIRATORY SYSTEM 
No dyspnoea, wheeze 
Position of trachea:central 
Breathing sounds: vesicular 
ABDOMEN 
Shape of abdomen:scaphoid 
No tenderness,palpable mass, free fluid, bruits,bowel sounds 
Normal hernial orifices 
Liver and spleen not palpable 
CNS 
Level of consciousness:conscious 
Speech:normal 
CLINICAL FINDINGS
Sloitary, oval swelling located at medial aspect of right knee joint . The
surface of swelling is smooth the swelling was non pulsatile . There was no
local rise of temperature, firm in consistency,was freely movable and there was
no fixation to underlying skin . Skin over the swelling was pinchable 
PROVISIONAL DIAGNOSIS 
Lipoma over medial aspect of right knee 
Medication:
Tab.pan40mg 
Tab.MVT
Patient was advised to have excision
