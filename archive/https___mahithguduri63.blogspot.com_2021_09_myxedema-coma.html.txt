Case Discussion

The main mechanism for the development of hyponatremia in patients with chronic
hypothyroidism is the decreased capacity of free water excretion due to
elevated antidiuretic hormone levels, which are mainly attributed to the
hypothyroidism-induced decrease in cardiac output.
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27484454/


25% of patients with primary aldosteronism can have normal Potassium .

Primary aldosteronism (15-20%) is commoner than pheochromocytoma (0.6%)
